Chapter 1: Introduction to Engineering Management

Before an engineer can manage structures, he must first manage himself. This chapter covers the role of engineers as managers, their qualifications, and the keys to success in engineering management.

Chapter 2: Decision-Making

The destination we reach depends on the path we take. In engineering management, the success of innovative solutions depends on the decisions of an engineering manager. Therefore, a solid understanding of effective decision-making is essential for managing engineering projects.

Chapter 3: Managing Product and Service Operations

Money is like a plant, you shower it with proper funding process, then it grows. But if you’re just consuming it without careful plan, it dies. As a result, engineering managers should consider planning, organizing, directing, and controlling financial activities to make sure that the company will reach stability and growth in the long run. In this process, they focuses on proper resource allocation, credible funding sources, and ensuring compliance with financial regulations.
